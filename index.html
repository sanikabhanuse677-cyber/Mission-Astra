PHASE 01 // BRIEFING

Mission Briefing

Familiarize yourself with the controls and stage objectives before launch.

Controls

  • ↑ Thrust
  • ← Rotate Left
  • → Rotate Right
  • SPACE Action / Tap

Tip: On touch devices, on-screen controls will appear during gameplay.

Stage Tips

  • Launch: Maintain stability against tilt.
  • Orbit Raising: Use precise timing.
  • Trans-Lunar Injection: Set correct trajectory.
  • Lunar Orbit Insertion: Reduce speed at the right moment.
  • Final Descent: Control your descent carefully.
Objective: Complete all mission phases to successfully land the Astra Lander.

Stage-by-Stage Walkthrough

Exactly what to do in each phase. Tap a card to expand.

01 Launch Phase Goal: keep the rocket vertical until it clears the atmosphere
  1. Hold ↑ to fire the main engine — the rocket starts climbing immediately.
  2. Watch the tilt indicator at the top of the canvas. Wind will push the rocket left or right.
  3. Tap ← or → in short bursts to counter the tilt. Don't over-correct.
  4. Keep tilt within the safe range. If it exceeds the limit (about ±25°), the rocket breaks up.
  5. Reach the target altitude bar at the top of the screen — stage clears automatically.

Pro tip: Pulse the rotation keys instead of holding them. Two quick taps usually beats one long press.

02 Orbit Raising Goal: complete 4 timed burns to widen your orbit
  1. You'll see a moving marker sweeping across a horizontal bar.
  2. A green window sits in the middle — that's the optimal burn zone.
  3. Press SPACE (or tap the screen) the moment the marker is inside the green zone.
  4. A successful tap raises your orbit (counter goes up). A miss costs you and resets the marker.
  5. Land 4 successful burns and the stage completes.

Pro tip: Don't chase the marker — anticipate it. Tap slightly before it enters the zone, since reaction time is real.

03 Trans-Lunar Injection Goal: aim a trajectory line into the Moon, then commit
  1. A dotted trajectory line extends from your spacecraft, with the Moon shown in the distance.
  2. Use ← / → to rotate the angle until the line passes through the Moon target.
  3. Hold ↑ to charge thrust power — you'll see the power bar fill up. Aim for the green band.
  4. Once your aim looks good and power is in the green, press SPACE to commit the burn.
  5. Too low: you fall back to Earth. Too high: you overshoot. Just right: you arrive at the Moon.

Pro tip: Set your aim first, then build power. Trying to do both at once usually misses both.

04 Lunar Orbit Insertion Goal: brake at the right moment to be captured by lunar gravity
  1. Your spacecraft approaches the Moon at high speed — too fast to be captured naturally.
  2. A green capture window appears as you near the Moon. This is your braking opportunity.
  3. Hold ↑ to fire your retro-thrusters the moment you enter the green window.
  4. Keep braking until your speed drops into the safe capture range (shown on the speed gauge).
  5. Brake too early — wasted fuel. Too late — you fly past the Moon. Inside the window is success.

Pro tip: Listen for the audio cue when the window opens — your eyes will be busy watching the speed gauge.

05 Final Descent Goal: land softly on the pad — vertical speed under 2.0 m/s
  1. You start with limited fuel. Watch the fuel gauge — running out mid-descent means crashing.
  2. Use ← / → to rotate. Use ↑ to thrust against gravity.
  3. Steer toward the highlighted landing pad on the surface — landing anywhere else is a fail.
  4. Stay nearly vertical as you approach. A tilted touchdown tips the lander over.
  5. Slow down so your vertical speed is under 2.0 m/s at touchdown. The HUD shows your current Vy.
  6. Touch down gently, upright, on the pad — mission complete.

Pro tip: Fall fast at first to save fuel, then "suicide burn" — full throttle late — to slow just before landing. Practice this on Easy first.
